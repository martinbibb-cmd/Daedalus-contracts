# Canonical Ontology Direction

Daedalus Contracts owns the canonical ontology direction for Daedalus.

The ontology names the kinds of reality Daedalus is allowed to model and the relationships between them.

It does not describe implementation.

It does not replace the Constitution, Manifesto, Laws or Philosophy Maintenance.

It derives from them.

## Authority

Canonical concepts begin in Daedalus Contracts.

Other repositories may project, refine or implement these concepts.

They must not redefine them.

Main may develop domain ontology refinements for reasoning, physics, services and explanations, but those refinements must remain traceable to canonical concepts owned here.

## Canonical Concept Families

The canonical ontology is organised around these concept families:

- Property: the root identity.
- Twin: the source of truth for modelled reality.
- House: the physical building, spaces, fabric, orientation, location and structure.
- Systems: the technical systems, components, relationships, services, capabilities and states.
- Home: the people, goals, routines, accessibility needs, preferences and lived context.
- Evidence: source material attached to reality.
- Observation: what has been seen, measured or recorded.
- Inference: what follows from evidence and model structure.
- Simulation: what is calculated from an explicit model.
- Projection: a report, visualisation, conversation, narration, AR view, PDF or future interface rendered from the Twin.
- Scenario: a possible future state of the Twin.
- Transformation: the movement from current twin to changed elements to future twin.
- Goal: what the Home is trying to achieve.
- Constraint: what reality allows or prevents.
- Challenge: what implementation must resolve.
- Capability: what a component, system or configuration can support.
- Consequence: what follows from a modelled state or scenario.
- Uncertainty: unknown, approximate, inferred, simulated or unresolved state.
- Provenance: the trace from model state back to evidence, source or derivation.

## Refinement Rules

Domain refinements must derive from canonical concepts.

A refinement may make a concept more precise for a domain.

A refinement must not change the meaning of the parent concept.

For example:

- A hydraulic loop may refine System relationship and capability.
- A heat source may refine System component.
- A delivery blockage may refine Constraint or Challenge depending on whether it is imposed by reality or implementation.
- A customer report may refine Projection.

If a domain refinement appears to introduce a new canonical concept, it must be proposed back to Daedalus Contracts before other repositories depend on it.

## Non-Redefinition Rule

No repository may redefine canonical concepts locally.

Main must not create a second meaning for Twin, Projection, Scenario, Transformation, Goal, Constraint, Challenge, Evidence, Observation, Inference or Simulation.

If Main needs a domain-specific concept, it must state which canonical concept it refines.

## State Separation

Observed, inferred and simulated states are different.

Unknown, inferred and simulated states must remain visibly distinct.

Projected views must not collapse those states into false certainty.

## Projection Boundary

Every projection is rendered from the Twin.

No projection owns truth.

No projection may invent model state.

No projection may hide uncertainty.

No projection may alter the Twin except through a governed update.

## Canon Maintenance

Ontology changes are Canon changes.

Before adding or renaming a canonical concept, apply Daedalus Philosophy Maintenance:

- Check the Constitution.
- Check the Manifesto.
- Check the Laws.
- Check existing ontology direction.
- Determine whether the concept already exists, is implied, or is genuinely new.

Prefer deriving rather than inventing.
