# The Laws of Daedalus

Philosophical changes are governed by [Daedalus Philosophy Maintenance](PHILOSOPHY_MAINTENANCE.md).

## Introduction

The Constitution defines the boundaries of Daedalus.

The Manifesto explains its purpose.

The Laws describe the immutable truths from which every design decision, feature, model and explanation should naturally emerge.

The Laws do not describe implementation.

They describe reality.

A future engineer should be able to evaluate a proposed feature simply by asking:

- Does it violate the Constitution?
- Does it violate a Law?
- If not, which Law does it express?

## Law 1

Reality precedes explanation.

Daedalus must never explain what has not first been modelled.

---

## Law 2

The Digital Twin is the Product.

Reports, AI, visualisations and conversations are views of the Twin.

The Twin owns the truth.

---

## Law 3

A home consists of three interacting realities.

- House
- Systems
- Home

Understanding emerges from their interaction.

---

## Law 4

Unknown is a valid state.

Missing information must never be replaced by invention.

Daedalus prefers uncertainty to false certainty.

---

## Law 5

Observed, inferred and simulated are different.

- Observed reality
- Inference
- Simulation

These states must never be presented as equivalent.

---

## Law 6

Components define capability.

Relationships define behaviour.

Physics emerges from their interaction.

---

## Law 7

Capture maximises information gain.

Capture is not measured by evidence quantity.

Capture is measured by how much uncertainty is removed.

---

## Law 8

The model owns the explanation.

Visualisations, reports, conversations, accessibility features and future interfaces are simply renderers of the same underlying model.

---

## Law 9

Compare futures, not products.

Daedalus explains how possible futures differ.

It does not recommend products.

---

## Law 10

Goals belong to the Home.

Constraints belong to reality.

Challenges belong to implementation.

Human decisions balance all three.

---

## Law 11

The cleanest line to understanding is the correct design.

Every feature should reduce the distance between the user and understanding.

---

## Law 12

Daedalus is objective.

Most sales handles objections.

Daedalus models reality.

The user remains responsible for the decision.

## Future Work

These Laws are expected to evolve as deeper principles are discovered.

When a new Law is added, it should clarify an underlying truth that was already present in the system.

It should not describe a feature, justify an implementation, or weaken an existing Law.
